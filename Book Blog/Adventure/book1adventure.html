Hatchet

by Gary Paulsen

Genre: Adventure, Survival Fiction

Published: 1987

Rating: ★★★★☆ (4.5/5)

Synopsis:

Thirteen-year-old Brian Robeson is traveling by single-engine plane to visit his father when the plane crashes in the Canadian wilderness. With nothing but his clothing, a tattered windbreaker, and the hatchet his mother gave him as a present, Brian must learn to survive on his own.

As Brian faces the challenges of finding food, making shelter, and protecting himself from wild animals, he undergoes an incredible personal transformation. The hatchet becomes his most valuable tool and his only companion in this gripping tale of survival and self-discovery.

Why Read It?

Hatchet is a classic survival story that captures the imagination of readers young and old. Gary Paulsen's vivid writing puts you right in the wilderness with Brian, making you feel every triumph and setback. It's a story about resilience, problem-solving, and the incredible strength of the human spirit.
